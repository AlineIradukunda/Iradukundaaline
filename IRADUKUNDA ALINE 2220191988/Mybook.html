    • Customer table

      • This table stores information about the restaurant's customers, such as their names, contact details, address, and order history. Keeping track of customer information can help the restaurant provide personalized service and targeted marketing campaigns.


    • Employee table

      • This table stores information about the restaurant employees, including their names, contact details, job titles, address, and possibly their work schedules or shifts. There might be separate tables for different types of staff, such as servers, chefs, and managers.


    • Menu table

      • This table contains information about the menu items offered by the restaurant, such as their names and price (e.g., appetizers, main courses, desserts). Each menu item might also have additional attributes like ingredients, allergen information, and nutritional values.


    • Order table

      • This table records information about customer orders, including the date and time the order was placed, the table or customer who placed the order, and any special instructions or requests. Each order might also have a unique order ID for easy reference.


    • Reservation table

If the restaurant accepts reservations, this table will store information about upcoming reservations, including the date and time, the number of guests, any special requests, and contact details for the person making the reservation.
